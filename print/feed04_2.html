MAKE KIN by Aude Jomini and Sarah Fritchey
 Published as part of Silence Breakers,  March - April, 2018 at Ely Center for Contemporary Art.
Description from exhibition: These posters are to be written on, in kinship or protest and are free to take away.
Donna Haraway's truly female point of view, creative and twisted, is at the same time optimistic and completely anti-cynical.
It is, to us, a way of engaging with our time of crisis; an approach which is just altogether OTHER...
what seems to stand in the way. It is feminist, it is radical, and it is utterly contemporary.
We must think with the contingency of actors and actions described by our fragile interconnected worlding:
"It matters what matters we use to think other matters with; it matters what stories we tell to tell other stories with;
it matters what knots knot knots, what thoughts think thoughts,
what descriptions describe descriptions, what tie tie ties..‚Äù
Donna J. Haraway
Reference: Donna J Haraway: Staying with the Trouble: Making Kin in the Chthulucene


 

FEED IS A COLLECTIVE PROJECT

FEED is a collective project and curatorial platform. We are artists based around  New Haven Connecticut.  We come together to engage inter-disciplinary modes of work, to collaborate and create new artworks, physical and digital, to produce print projects, craft proposals, engage new modes of discourse, and participate in the communities we love.  
FEED is a group of collaborators open to larger participation.  

FEED is  Aude Jomini, Eben Kling, Sarah Fritchey, Charli Taylor,
with project-specific contributions by other artists.
